Why Code?????!?

So why am I making this change of career?

There are two factors, one pushing me from my old career and one pulling me to coding specifically.

The Pull: I have wanted to make software, websites and games since I was a child. I was never taught to code, and found the idea incredibly intimidating. During lockdown, I discovered that code made sense to me... partly because my ADHD diagnosis & treatment meant that I now had the ability to intentionally focus on complex detail.

The Push: For 15 years, I have simultaneously won awards & praise whilst simultaneously struggling to pay my bills; at times, I have only survived through the generosity & love of my family. I want to work in a field where I can both create some value in the world and, just maybe, be paid a salary.

Weaver

My mother's uncle passed away with dementia a while back. I was lucky enough to talk to him a fair bit towards the end of his time.

I discovered that he was able to have superb conversations with us, as long as I kept anchoring the topic in a detail from his deeper past.

Weaver would be a digital tool for those with dementia and their loved ones, designed to organically create a personalised digital tool that facilitates communication between them.

Hyperfocus

One of the lesser-known symptoms of ADHD is "hyperfocus".

If we find something that starts releasing dopamine in our brain, we become completely oblivious to anything else.

You can see this in someone with ADHD who plays a video game for 7 hours and doesn't realise they need the toilet until they've nearly done something socially unacceptable.

But once we learn how to harness it, it's incredibly powerful.

For example, I wrote the dissertation for my Master's Degree in two nights. This is not a healthy approach, but did have 3 interesting outcomes:

  1. I got a Distinction
  2. It formed the basis of my book
  3. I am single-handedly responsible for that course now having a maximum word limit on dissertations

Distributor

An web-app for educational institutions.

Often a student has a valid exemption from a general rule... but the educator doesn't know this. The student is forced to explain their reasons.

Distributor would take data that has been input by Safeguarding & Administration, and output exemptions to the educators without context. I don't need to know why this person is allowed a toilet break even during their GCSE.
